This research direction explores the integration of virtual reality technology with physiological signal monitoring, including:

## Electroencephalography (EEG)

- **Brain-Computer Interfaces (BCI)**: Using EEG signals to control VR environments
- **Cognitive Load Assessment**: Monitoring mental workload during VR tasks
- **Emotion Recognition**: Detecting emotional states through brain activity patterns
- **Attention Tracking**: Measuring focus and engagement in virtual environments

## Electromyography (EMG)

- **Muscle Activity Monitoring**: Tracking muscle activation during VR exercises
- **Gesture Recognition**: Interpreting hand and body gestures from muscle signals
- **Rehabilitation Assessment**: Evaluating motor function recovery
- **Fatigue Detection**: Monitoring muscle fatigue during prolonged VR use

## Applications

- **Medical Rehabilitation**: VR-based therapy with real-time physiological feedback
- **Training and Simulation**: Adaptive VR environments based on user state
- **Gaming**: Responsive game mechanics based on player physiology
- **Research Tools**: Novel methods for studying human behavior in controlled virtual environments
